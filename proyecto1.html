VIVIENDAS BERLÍN

danielespanon

“Viviendas Berlín” is a social housing project that engages with the notion of boundaries: physical, social, and urban. Positioned between two contrasting neighbourhoods, the intervention responds with a linear volume that frees up public green space. The architecture aims to mediate between communities while remaining sensitive to its environmental context.

The building aligns with the site's perimeter, creating shaded patios and transitional terraces that extend domestic life outdoors. Housing units, tailored to a defined user group, are composed of functional volumetric modules arranged in varied configurations. A commercial plinth activates the street level, fostering local commerce and inter-neighbourhood connection. Strategic perforations within the built mass ensure visual and spatial continuity, reinforcing the project’s intent to remain permeable—both physically and socially—within its urban setting.
The green space, liberated by the building's linear configuration, is conceived not as a residual zone but as a sequence of ecological rooms. Each sub-zone is defined by autochthonous vegetation, selected for its capacity to interact meaningfully with the built environment; providing shade, enhancing cross-ventilation, and ensuring visual porosity. This landscape strategy establishes a microclimatic buffer that contributes to the comfort and sustainability of the dwellings. Moreover, the use of local plant species reduces maintenance needs and supports biodiversity, reinforcing a sense of place. The garden becomes both a climatic device and a civic space of encounter and continuity.
