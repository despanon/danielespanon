LA CAJA DE LOS ÁNGELES

danielespanon

La caja is a threshold: a place that is not exactly indoors nor entirely outdoors. Its own structure supports a series of movable enclosures drawn from the imagery and vernacular architecture of the neighborhood. Shutters, latticework, grilles, awnings, and translucent panels are reconfigured into a living system that responds to the climate. Like a breathing skin that opens and closes according to the needs of the weather and its inhabitants.

Like the Ark of the Covenant, la caja de los ángeles contains the sacred. But it does not hold the stone tablets of the Ten Commandments, rather, something just as sacred: everyday life, relationships, the hum of the neighborhood. This covering rests upon two facing low-rise buildings, creating a suspended volume, a second skin, that unites them and transcends them.

The project also breathes new life into the existing library and civic center, weaving them into a renewed dialogue with the nearby school. Part of the building is transformed into a new library and a versatile gathering space, together forming a sheltered threshold between the playground and the worlds of study. Beneath this continuous canopy, movement and pause intertwine. Children crossing paths with readers, neighbors with students, opening the way for new encounters, shared moments, and a richer tapestry of daily life.
