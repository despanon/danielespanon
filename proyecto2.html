SAN FIACRE COLLEGE

danielespanon

This residential project and its expansion adopt a perimeter-based configuration, orienting the built mass outward and opening the core of the site to form a shared green void. By liberating the ground floor and the central portion of the plot, the proposal generates a porous, accessible courtyard that serves as a space for encounter, rest, and social interaction; not only for the building's users but for the broader urban community of Rome.

The student residence, composed of shared living units, is strategically located on the rooftop of the building, granting it enhanced privacy and a privileged vantage point within the urban fabric. The academic program unfolds along a longitudinal volume articulated by fluid, open-plan classrooms. Elevated horizontal circulations hover above these teaching spaces, generating visual connections and layered spatial experiences. This spatial configuration promotes a new paradigm of education, one that is open, adaptable, and collaborative, transforming the college into a dynamic environment that fosters both individual focus and collective learning.

Conceived as a flexible, multi-use public realm, this landscaped interior is sheltered by the architectural envelope, offering thermal comfort, spatial intimacy, and a sense of civic inclusion. The ground floor structure articulates the open space by subtly compartmentalizing it into a series of spatial zones or “outdoor rooms” that support and activate the central courtyard. These defined sub-spaces respond to programmatic needs while maintaining visual and physical continuity.
The courtyard is carefully landscaped with native plant species, selected not only for their ecological value but also for their capacity to enrich the sensory experience of the space. Their placement is strategically informed by solar orientation and soil conditions, ensuring environmental responsiveness and seasonal comfort.
